"Tapestry of Nostalgia"

Stitching Together Memories

Prev         Next

Oil pastels on paper, dimensions 60 x 42cm

This oil painting is my tribute to the kitschy Turkish tapestries that hung on the walls of an Romanian grandmother home, a staple in so many working-class homes after communism. These tapestries, with their vibrant scenes of lavish women gathered around fountains, enjoying coffee and cigarettes in lush gardens, were windows into a fantasy world—a splash of color and luxury in homes that often felt plain.

They carpeted the backdrop of my childhood, woven into everyday life and memory. In this piece, I tried to recreate that worn-out tapestry, but in my own naive, rule-free style. It’s not about precision or perfect representation—it’s about capturing the nostalgia and warmth of those times, when these tapestries were symbols of aspiration and a world beyond the grey realities of post-communism.
